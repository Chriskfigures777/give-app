Slide 4

The Exchange brings everything into one place. Let me walk through what that actually means.

Giving and donations, organizations can accept tithes, one-time gifts, and recurring giving with donor receipts and giving history built in. Joint shared giving lets two connected organizations automatically split incoming donations between them. Payment transfers let funds move directly through the network.

Website builder and custom forms, churches can build and host their own site and create donation or intake forms without touching code.

Flock management is the CRM layer, leaders track members, flag who is active, and know who needs follow-up. AI notes turn sermon or meeting content into automatic engagement surveys. Broadcasts let them reach their entire list by SMS or email from the same dashboard.

There is also a budget sheet for both individuals and organizations, completely free, so members and staff can track income, expenses, and giving all in one place. And payroll processing is coming around Month 6 for churches with staff.

Most organizations today use five or more separate tools to cover what this platform does. This is not a collection of features. It is one operating system for the faith community.


Morgan
Slide 5

Three capabilities we have not found combined together in any platform we researched.

AI Notes to Engagement, where pastor notes become auto-generated surveys that identify active, middle, and inactive members automatically, so no one slips through the cracks.

Joint Shared Giving, where organizations auto-split tithes with connected partners on every donation.

And the Exchange Banking Ecosystem, with FDIC accounts, debit cards, peer-to-peer transfers, and bill pay inside the same platform your giving flows through.

Slide 6

Underneath every feature on this platform is a social network. That is intentional.

Today, anyone on the Exchange can search for a church, a nonprofit, or a cause they believe in, and donate directly from the results. Organizations post updates to a community feed. Members interact, share, and stay connected to the mission in between services.

Two features drive organic growth at zero cost. Connect forms, where a pastor shares their in-app connect survey and members sign up directly through it. Every form completed is a new platform user with no ad spend required. And AI-generated surveys, where after a pastor saves sermon notes the platform automatically sends follow-up survey questions to the congregation. Members stay on the platform. Engagement stays measurable without anyone doing it manually.

Real-time funds arrive from people who are willing to give. No public post. No shame. No waiting for a government program. The Body of Christ, taking care of its own, through infrastructure that makes it fast and private.

Post boosting is also on the roadmap. Organizations can boost a post for ten to twenty dollars to reach a wider audience inside the Exchange network. That is a new revenue stream that does not require a third-party ad platform.

And the mobile app, dedicated iOS and Android focused on the social and discovery experience, targets a launch of six to eight months from the close of this round.

Marcus
Slide 11

This is how we go to market.

Our rule is simple: earn proof before spending money. We start with direct outreach and in-person church visits at zero ad cost. One to three pilot churches validate the product. We capture a testimonial video and a one-page success story. And during this entire phase, the platform is already driving organic growth through connect forms and AI-generated surveys. Every pastor who shares a connect form brings new users onto the platform at zero cost.

Only then do we start testing ads. Phase 2 is controlled testing at $1,000 a month on Facebook. We do not scale anything until the data shows it is working. What working looks like will be defined by what we observe, not a pre-set number.

Phase 3 scales proven ads from $3,000 to $18,000 a month across Facebook and YouTube. Phase 4 activates a faith-based advisor referral network once sufficient audience exists. That is our path.


Marcus
Slide 12

Facebook takes 65% of ad spend. YouTube takes 35%.

We test at $1,000 a month first. Facebook targets pastors, church administrators, and ministry leaders. YouTube runs 15 and 30 second pre-roll ads against church management and ministry finance channels.

We A/B test one variable at a time. Hook copy first. Then creative format. Then audience. Then the call to action. Once two or more ad sets are confirmed, we ramp the budget.

Our budget gates are not negotiable. Cost-per-lead must be below $80. Trial-to-paid must be above 25%. Trial start rate must be above 30%. Meet the gates, then scale. Total 24-month paid ad budget is approximately $200,000.


Marcus
Slide 13

Our advertising strategy is simple: we do not spread ourselves across every platform at once.

We start on Facebook. That is it. We master Facebook before we move anywhere else. The reason is not that other platforms are bad. The reason is that every platform requires its own creative format, its own audience segmentation, its own testing logic, and its own optimization discipline. If you try to do all of them at the same time with a limited budget and a small team, you delay results everywhere. A divided focus costs time, and time costs money.

Facebook gives us the best targeting precision available anywhere. We can target by job title — Executive Pastor, Church Administrator, Ministry Director — and reach the exact decision-maker inside the church. We test at $1,000 a month. We prove cost-per-lead under $80. We prove trial-to-paid above 25%. Then and only then do we scale.

Phase 2 adds YouTube. YouTube pre-roll at five to ten cents per view is the most cost-efficient format for a software product demo. The audience watching church management and ministry finance content on YouTube is already in the mindset we need. We activate YouTube after Facebook is proven and generating positive return on ad spend.

Phase 3 and beyond is when we add other platforms — TikTok at the cheapest CPM of any major platform, faith network placements on Gospel Coalition and Bible sites, and eventually LinkedIn for enterprise-level church outreach. Each one is added only after the previous channel is self-funding.

One proven platform funds the next. This is not a limitation. This is discipline. And this strategy is subject to change based on what the data tells us.


Marcus
Slide 14

Every dollar we spend on ads has one primary goal: getting a church leader to start a 14-day free trial.

Not an impression. Not a click. A trial start.

Here is the funnel. One thousand people see the ad. Twenty to thirty click through to the website, roughly a two to three percent click-through rate. Of those visitors, approximately fifteen percent start a 14-day trial — that is three to five trial starts per thousand impressions. Of those trials, our target is that at least twenty-five percent convert to a paid plan. That is roughly one paid organization per thousand impressions, at a cost of forty to eighty dollars per trial start on Facebook.

The primary ad creative is the website builder. The hook is simple: build your church website in ten minutes, free 14-day trial, no credit card required. That is a tangible, immediate, and zero-risk offer for any church admin who has been putting off their website for months.

The secondary hook is the giving platform. Try online giving for your church — free forever. That CTA targets churches that do not have digital giving set up yet. Both ads drive to a dedicated landing page with one action only.

We do not know our exact cost per trial start until the ads are running. What we know is that Facebook and YouTube are the most cost-efficient platforms for reaching church administrators and pastors at scale, and TikTok becomes the lowest-CPM option when we expand in Phase 3. We measure everything and scale only what the data supports.


Marcus
Slide 15

Email is our most cost-efficient acquisition layer and it runs seven behavioral sequences.

Sequence one is for new leads from website signups or ad landing pages: three emails in seven days moving from platform intro to pilot church proof story to a free trial invite. The pilot survey work is done in-person and foot traffic only — that intake stays internal and separate from the email engine. Sequence two is newsletter subscribers who receive monthly church stewardship content.

Sequences three through five are upgrade paths. Free accounts get a $29 upgrade sequence at Day 7, 14, 30, and 60. $29 accounts get a $49 sequence at Day 30, 45, and 65. $49 accounts get a $89 payroll sequence when that plan activates. Banking is not part of this $89 upsell and remains a separate free product layer.

Sequence six is trial nurture: Day 1, 3, 7, and 13, with a 7-day extension offer on Day 15 if no conversion. Sequence seven is win-back for churned users at Day 30, 60, and 90.

Every email is behavioral, not calendar-based. The platform built this on Brevo at $25 to $150 a month.

Track 1 targets creators with 5,000 to 50,000 subscribers in church administration and ministry finance. We offer a permanently free Pro plan in exchange for an honest review video. No scripted talking points. The platform earns the mention.

Trusted creators convert at a dramatically higher rate than paid ads. That is the point.

Slide 17

Once a church adopts the full platform, keeping them is effortless. The switching cost becomes enormous.

In-app upgrade nudges fire at exactly the right moment. ROI-framing copy appears at every locked feature gate, not just a paywall. Usage-limit banners appear at 80% of the limit, not 100%. The third survey triggers a CRM upsell. The third staff member triggers a payroll upsell.

And the financial lock-in stack compounds over time. The church website is here. The payroll is here. The banking account is here. The donor history is here. Moving means rebuilding all of it.

Our monthly churn target is under 5% in Phase 1 and under 3% by Phase 3. Every church that adopts website, payroll, and banking together is estimated to have near-zero churn.

That is the retention engine.


Marcus
Slide 18

Once a church converts, whether to the free plan or a paid plan, we have three internal tiers that move them forward. No additional ad spend required.

Tier one is in-app milestones and celebrations. When a church processes their first donation, we congratulate them. When they send their first survey, we celebrate it. When they add their fifth staff member, we celebrate that too and show them the payroll plan. The tone is always celebration first, upgrade second. We never lead with a wall.

Tier two is SMS and email outreach. Day 3 is a welcome text. Day 7 is an email showing them what they built that week. Day 14 highlights one feature they have not used yet. Day 30 sends a monthly stewardship summary with ROI framing for the next tier. If we see usage drop for three days, a check-in text goes out automatically.

Tier three is customer success calls. Every free plan church gets a personal call within their first 14 days. Not a sales call. A ministry call. We ask how things are going, what they are trying to accomplish, what is blocking them. We walk them through one feature they have not discovered yet. If three or more of their goals align with a paid tier, we present it naturally.

We estimate 15 to 25 percent of free users convert to paid within 90 days through this channel alone. That is our internal conversion engine.
