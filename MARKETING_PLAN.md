## Executive Summary

The Exchange App is a faith-based SaaS platform built for churches, ministries, and Christian nonprofits. This marketing plan covers the full acquisition and retention strategy from pre-launch surveys through mobile app release, organized into phases across 24 months.

**Core marketing principle:** Earn trust before spending money. Pilot proof → tested ads → scale → influencer program → mobile app.

## Phase 0 — Survey, Foot Traffic & Organic Platform Growth (Month 0–1)

**Goal:** Collect 50–100 church/nonprofit contacts, onboard pilot churches, and activate the platform's built-in organic growth tools before spending a dollar on ads.

### Approach

Direct in-person outreach to churches, nonprofits, ministry events, and faith-based conferences. Every in-person conversation ends with a QR code or text link to a Fillout.com intake form.

### Survey Tool: Fillout.com (In-Person Pilot Intake Only)

**Important:** Fillout.com is used only for in-person and foot-traffic outreach during the pilot phase. It is not used for general email marketing or for website leads — those flow through the platform's own signup and Brevo behavioral sequences.

Use **Fillout.com** (free tier) for pilot intake. Advantages:
- Multi-step conditional logic (e.g., show payroll questions only if church has staff)
- Branded form design
- Email capture built in
- Free tier supports unlimited responses

**Survey questions:**
1. What tools does your church currently use for donations? (multi-select)
2. Does your church have a website? If yes, which platform?
3. How many paid staff does your church employ?
4. What is your biggest administrative pain point? (open-ended)
5. Would a free trial of an all-in-one church platform interest you?
6. Your name and email (for follow-up)

### Foot Traffic Tactics

- Visit 3–5 local churches in person weekly; ask to speak with pastor or administrator
- Attend local faith-based networking events, nonprofit meetups, ministry conferences
- Carry a one-page "what is The Exchange?" flyer with QR code to Fillout intake form
- Bring a tablet/laptop for a live 5-minute demo of the website builder and donation form

### Outcome

- 50–100 intake responses → pilot church candidates identified
- Responses loaded into Brevo under a "pilot outreach" segment
- Identify 1–3 churches willing to be pilot users
- No paid ads until pilot proof exists

### Built-In Organic Growth (Runs Concurrently — Zero CAC)

The platform itself generates new users from day one through two organic mechanisms:

**Connect Forms** — When a pastor or church admin shares their in-app connect survey link (via text, social media, or Sunday bulletin), members join the platform by filling it out. Every completed form is a new registered user at zero cost.

**AI-Generated Surveys** — After a pastor saves sermon or meeting notes, the platform automatically generates relevant survey questions and broadcasts them to the congregation. Members return to the platform to respond. Engagement is tracked automatically; inactive members are flagged for pastoral follow-up. This keeps members on the platform between services and creates a self-reinforcing usage loop.

These two tools mean every pilot church that onboards immediately starts growing the platform organically.

**Cost:** $0 (Fillout free tier, founder time only)

## Phase 2 — Ad Testing: $1,000/mo Test Budget (Month 2–4)

**Goal:** Find one proven ad set before scaling spend.

### The Test-First Principle

Start on Facebook only at $1,000/mo. Do not add YouTube or other platforms until Facebook is producing results. Do not scale until at least one ad set is consistently profitable. Scaling an unproven ad wastes capital.

### Why Facebook First

Each platform requires its own creative format, audience behavior, and optimization logic. Dividing attention and budget across multiple platforms simultaneously produces weaker results on every channel. Facebook is the starting point because it offers the best job-title targeting for reaching pastors, church administrators, and ministry directors directly. Once Facebook is proven, YouTube is added as the video demo channel.

### A/B Test Variables (Run Sequentially — One Variable at a Time)

**Test 1 — Hook Copy:**
- Version A: "Start your church website free"
- Version B: "The all-in-one platform your church has been missing"
- Version C: "Run your church's giving, website, and budget from one place"

**Test 2 — Creative Format:**
- Version A: 60-second testimonial video (pilot church pastor)
- Version B: 90-second product demo (screen recording of website builder)
- Version C: Static image with stat ("380,000 US churches. Most use 5+ disconnected tools.")

**Test 3 — Audience Targeting (Facebook):**
- Audience A: Pastors, church administrators, ministry leaders (job title targeting)
- Audience B: Nonprofit directors, faith-based business owners
- Audience C: Retargeting — anyone who visited the landing page but didn't sign up

**Test 4 — CTA / Landing Page:**
- CTA A: "Start Free — No Credit Card"
- CTA B: "Watch a 2-Minute Demo"
- CTA C: "Try the Website Builder Free for 14 Days"

### Scale Discipline

Do NOT scale past $3,000/mo until the data shows consistent, repeatable results across at least two winning ad variations. What "working" looks like will be defined by the actual numbers observed — not pre-set targets. The goal is to find proof before pouring money in.

**Analytics tools:**
- Facebook Ads Manager (primary)
- Microsoft Clarity (free) — heat maps and session recordings on landing page
- Easy Heatmap — click tracking on specific CTA areas
- Supabase — track signups, trial starts, plan activations, feature adoption

## Lifecycle Marketing — Internal Conversion (3 Tiers)

Once an organization is on the platform — free or paid — three internal channels move them toward greater engagement and higher-value plans without any additional ad spend.

### Tier 1 — In-App Milestones & Celebrations

When a church hits a meaningful moment in the app, we acknowledge it first and introduce an upgrade second. The tone is always encouragement before it is ever a sales prompt.

| Milestone | Response |
|---|---|
| First donation processed | Congratulate — "Your first donation is in." |
| First survey sent | Celebrate — "Your congregation received your first survey." |
| 5th staff member added | Acknowledge + surface payroll plan in context |
| $500 in giving processed | Celebrate + introduce Pro analytics |
| Trial Day 10 | "4 days left — here's what you've built. Don't lose it." |

### Tier 2 — Triggered SMS & Email

Behavioral, not calendar-based:
- Day 3: Welcome text — "Welcome to The Exchange. Need help getting started?"
- Day 7: Email summary of what they built in their first week
- Day 14: Highlight one feature they have not yet used
- Day 30: Monthly stewardship summary with context on what the next plan adds
- 3 days of inactivity: Automated check-in text — "How are things going? We're here if you need anything."

### Tier 3 — Customer Success Calls

Every free plan organization gets a personal outreach call within their first 14 days. The purpose is not to sell — it is to understand what they are trying to accomplish, remove obstacles, and make sure they are getting real value from the platform. If their goals align with a paid plan, it is mentioned naturally.

**Estimated impact:** A meaningful percentage of free plan organizations are expected to convert to paid within 90 days through this channel alone. This is the internal conversion engine.

## Budget Sheet — Free for Everyone (Marketing Differentiator)

The Exchange includes a full-featured budget sheet for both individuals and organizations at no cost. This is not a basic tool — it is a complete financial planning experience:

**For individuals:** Income tracking, fixed expenses, variable expenses, transaction log, net cash flow calculation. All 12 months generated at once. Analytics with year-over-year comparison. Excel export.

**For organizations:** Same full feature set, but shared across all admins and staff at the same church. The whole team sees and edits the same budget. Includes an analytics view with KPI cards and charts. Excel export suitable for board and treasurer presentations.

**Faith-native detail:** The default fixed expense rows include a line item for Tithe and Giving out of the box. This product was designed for this community from the ground up.

**Marketing application:** The budget sheet is a retention and acquisition tool. Free users who build a year of budget history in the platform face meaningful switching costs. It also deepens the daily-use habit — members log in for their budget, not just to give.

## YouTube Influencer Program (Month 6–18)

**Timing:** Only activate after 20–30 churches are actively using Exchange-built websites. Real examples are required.

### Why YouTube

The Exchange's target customer — church administrators and ministry leaders — responds to trusted, educational content. A 10-minute YouTube video from a trusted creator converts at a dramatically higher rate than a 30-second ad, with zero ongoing cost after production.

### Target Creator Profile

- **Platform:** YouTube (primary), podcast (secondary)
- **Niche:** Church administration, faith-based financial stewardship, ministry leadership, nonprofit management, Christian entrepreneurship
- **Subscriber range:** 5,000–500,000 (mid-tier converts better than mega-influencers for B2B SaaS)
- **NOT:** Generic Christian lifestyle, devotional, or sermon channels

### Two-Track Outreach Strategy

**Track 1 — Free Plan Offer (No Payment Required)**
For creators with 5K–50K subscribers or highly aligned audiences:
- Offer a permanently free Pro plan in exchange for an honest review video or mention
- No scripted talking points — the platform earns the mention
- Start here. Get 20–30 honest reviews before spending the paid sponsorship budget.

**Track 2 — Paid Sponsorship (After Organic Proof)**
For creators with 50K–500K subscribers:
- Only approach after Track 1 creators have published real videos
- Use those videos as evidence when pitching larger creators
- Paid sponsorship rate: $500–$3,000 per dedicated video or pre-roll mention
- Total budget: $12,000 (Months 10–18)

### Scale Target

- Month 6: Identify 100 top Christian YouTubers in target niches
- Month 7–9: Outreach to all 100 with Track 1 offer
- Month 9–12: 20–40 creators publish organic content
- Month 10–18: Pay 5–15 larger creators for dedicated sponsorships

**Projected outcome:** 50–200 new leads/month passively from YouTube, zero incremental ad spend after initial outreach.

## Retention Strategy

### Budget Sheet as Retention Mechanism

Churches and individual donors who build a full year of budget history inside the platform face meaningful switching costs. Goal tracking, monthly analytics, and year-over-year comparisons all compound over time. Moving to another tool means losing that history.

### Flock Tracking

After each sermon or ministry event, churches can send notes and surveys directly to their congregation. The platform shows engagement data — who responded, who is active, who needs outreach. This creates a member engagement record that becomes irreplaceable operational data.

### Financial Lock-In Stack

| Feature | Lock-In Mechanism |
|---|---|
| Website builder + custom domain | Moving means rebuilding the church's entire public web presence |
| Payroll processing | Moving means reconfiguring payroll workflows, compliance setup, and staff processes |
| Banking layer (Month 12+) | Church operating funds move inside the platform |
| Giving processing | All donor relationships and recurring giving tied to the platform |
| Budget sheet history | Years of monthly budgets, analytics, and exports are not portable |

### Community Feed & Peer Network

Churches can connect, share resources, support missionaries, and coordinate giving across organizations. As the network grows, it becomes more valuable — churches stay because other churches they know are here.

### Banking Layer & Peer-to-Peer Transfers (Month 12+)

When the banking layer launches around Month 12, donors can transfer funds to each other and to any church in the network. This makes The Exchange part of everyday financial life, not just Sunday morning, and provides a retention mechanism even for free-plan individual users.

## Social Network & Organic Discovery

The social network layer is not a future feature — it is live and central to the platform from day one. It serves both as a product differentiator and as an organic acquisition engine.

### What the Social Network Does Today

- **Community Feed** — Organizations post updates, events, campaigns, and ministry moments. Members engage and share.
- **Discover & Donate** — Any user can search churches, nonprofits, and causes by name, city, or category and donate directly from the search results.
- **Connect Forms** — In-app membership surveys let members join a church digitally. Every completed form is a new platform user with zero CAC.
- **AI-Generated Surveys** — Pastor notes auto-generate congregation surveys. Member engagement is tracked and scored automatically.

### The Vision: Anonymous Fund Requests

As the network grows, members will be able to submit anonymous fund requests (e.g., help with rent, medical bills) through their church. The church then broadcasts the need to their network, and funds flow in real time through the Exchange banking layer. No public shame. No government program required. The Body of Christ taking care of its own through digital infrastructure.

### Post Boosting (New Revenue Stream)

Organizations will be able to boost posts for $10–$20 to reach a wider audience inside the Exchange network. This creates an in-platform ad revenue stream that does not require a third-party ad network. It also incentivizes organizations to post more consistently, which compounds the social network value for all users.
